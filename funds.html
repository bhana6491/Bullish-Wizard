Bond Market

We already discussed the life cycle of a stock but let's dive a little bit deeper into what possessing a share really means. As a shareholder, you are obliged to participate in a company's wins and losses. The value of stocks is constantly fluctuating and it is up to you to decide when to enter and exit the market. Traditionally, if you sell a share when the price is higher than when you first bought it, you would realize a capital gain(profit). Looking at it from the other side, if you sell a share when the price is lower than when you first bought it, you would realize a capital loss(defecit). The possibility of capital gain is what makes investing in stocks so attractive to investors.
